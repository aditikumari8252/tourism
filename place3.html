About India Gate

India Gate is a war memorial located in the heart of New Delhi, India. The monument is 42 meters (138 feet) tall and was designed by Sir Edwin Lutyens. It was built in memory of the 70,000 Indian soldiers who died during World War I while fighting for the British Empire.

The monument is made of red sandstone and granite, and is surrounded by lush green lawns. The names of the soldiers who died during the war are inscribed on the walls of the monument.

Visit India Gate

If you want to visit India Gate, you can do so at any time of the day or night, as the monument is open 24 hours a day. There are no entry fees or tickets required to visit the monument. You can also enjoy a picnic or a leisurely walk on the lush green lawns surrounding the monument.

However, due to its popularity and significance, India Gate can get quite crowded, especially during weekends and holidays. It's recommended to visit early in the morning or late at night to avoid the crowds and to enjoy the serene atmosphere of the monument.
