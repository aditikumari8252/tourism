About Qutub Minar

The Qutub Minar is a towering landmark and UNESCO World Heritage Site located in Delhi, India. The tower is 73 meters (240 feet) tall and was built in the 12th century by Qutb-ud-din Aibak, the founder of the Delhi Sultanate.

The tower is made of red sandstone and marble, and is adorned with intricate carvings and inscriptions. It is surrounded by other ancient structures, including the Quwwat-ul-Islam Mosque and the Iron Pillar of Delhi.

Visit Qutub Minar

If you want to visit the Qutub Minar, you can book your tickets online through the official website of the Archaeological Survey of India. The monument is open to visitors every day from sunrise to sunset.
