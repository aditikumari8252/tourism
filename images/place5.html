About Red Fort

The Red Fort is a historic fort located in Old Delhi, India. It was built by the Mughal emperor Shah Jahan in the mid-17th century as his palace and the capital of the Mughal Empire. The fort is made of red sandstone and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

The Red Fort is famous for its impressive architecture, including the Diwan-i-Am (Hall of Public Audience), Diwan-i-Khas (Hall of Private Audience), and the Muthamman Burj (a tower where the emperor would address his subjects). The fort is also known for its beautiful gardens and intricate decorations.

History of Red Fort

The Red Fort was built by the Mughal emperor Shah Jahan in 1638. It served as the palace and capital of the Mughal Empire until 1857, when the British took control of India. During the Indian Rebellion of 1857, the fort was used as a military garrison by the British, and it continued to be used as a military base even after India gained independence in 1947.

Today, the Red Fort is a popular tourist attraction and is known for its beautiful architecture and historical significance.

Visit Red Fort

If you want to visit the Red Fort, you can do so at any time of the day except Mondays, as the fort is closed on Mondays. There is an entry fee for Indian and foreign visitors, and tickets can be purchased at the entrance gate. There are also audio guides available for an additional fee.

The Red Fort can get quite crowded, especially during weekends and holidays. It's recommended to visit early in the morning or late at night to avoid the crowds and to enjoy the peaceful atmosphere of the fort.
