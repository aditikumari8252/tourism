About Lotus Temple

Lotus Temple is a Bahá'í House of Worship located in New Delhi, India. The temple is shaped like a lotus flower and is made of white marble. It was designed by Iranian-Canadian architect Fariborz Sahba and was completed in 1986.

The temple is open to people of all religions and beliefs. It serves as a place of worship, meditation, and reflection, and is also a popular tourist attraction in Delhi.

Visit Lotus Temple

If you want to visit Lotus Temple, you can do so at any time of the day except Mondays, as the temple is closed on Mondays. There are no entry fees or tickets required to visit the temple. However, you are required to maintain silence inside the temple and to remove your shoes before entering the temple.

Lotus Temple can get quite crowded, especially during weekends and holidays. It's recommended to visit early in the morning or late at night to avoid the crowds and to enjoy the peaceful atmosphere of the temple.
